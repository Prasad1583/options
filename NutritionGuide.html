Understanding the significance of nutrients and consequences of deficiencies

üçûMacronutrients

üåæCarbohydrates

Significance:
  • Primary energy source for brain and central nervous system
  • Fuel for high-intensity physical activity
  • Spare protein from being used as energy
  • Support proper fat metabolism
  • Provide fiber for digestive health
Problems when lacking:
  • Ketosis and potential ketoacidosis
  • Mental fatigue and difficulty concentrating
  • Reduced athletic performance
  • Muscle protein breakdown for energy
  • Constipation (from lack of fiber)
  • Hypoglycemia symptoms
  • Bad breath and nausea

ü•©Proteins

Significance:
  • Building and repairing tissues
  • Making enzymes, hormones, and antibodies
  • Maintaining fluid and acid-base balance
  • Providing structure to cells and organs
  • Supporting immune function
  • Can serve as energy source when needed
Problems when lacking:
  • Muscle wasting and weakness
  • Slow wound healing
  • Increased susceptibility to infections
  • Hair loss and brittle nails
  • Edema (fluid retention)
  • Growth retardation in children
  • Kwashiorkor (severe protein deficiency)
  • Compromised immune system

ü•ëFats

Significance:
  • Energy storage and provision
  • Absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)
  • Cell membrane structure and function
  • Hormone production
  • Brain development and function
  • Insulation and organ protection
  • Essential fatty acid provision
Problems when lacking:
  • Essential fatty acid deficiency
  • Poor absorption of fat-soluble vitamins
  • Dry, scaly skin and hair
  • Impaired wound healing
  • Increased infection risk
  • Poor growth in children
  • Neurological problems
  • Hormone imbalances
